Hodgson, White and Wilson formed the band Runston Parva, a deliberate misspelling of a small [[East Riding of Yorkshire]] hamlet called [[Ruston Parva]]. After Runston Parva failed to secure a record deal, the group re-formed as Parva upon the return of Rix and Baines from university.\n\n===Parva and ''22'' (2000\u20132003)===\nParva's career went beyond the boundaries of Leeds, and the band was able to obtain both a record and publishing deal. However, after [[Beggars Banquet Records|Beggars Banquet]] closed the [[Mantra Recordings|Mantra]] label, Parva were dropped and without any direction after the release of an album (''22'') and three singles (\"Heavy\", \"Good Bad Right Wrong\" and \"Hessles\").\n\nAccording to manager James Sandom in an interview with [[HitQuarters]], as a dropped band they had become damaged goods, \"No one would touch them because they had a history. A lot of people used their history against them.\"<ref name=\"hitquarters.com\">{{cite web |url=http://www.hitquarters.com/index.php3?page=intrview/opar/intrview_James_Sandom_Interview.html |title=Interview with James Sandom |publisher=[[HitQuarters]]|date=28 May 2007 |access-date=17 November 2010}}</ref> The band decided that they would aim for a longer-term record deal and started afresh with new songs and a new name: Kaiser Chiefs. The new name was taken from South African football club [[Kaizer Chiefs F.C.|Kaizer Chiefs]], the first club of ex-[[Leeds United F.C.|Leeds United]] captain [[Lucas Radebe]].<ref name=\"Kaiser Chiefs Interview\">{{Cite web|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/southyorkshire/content/articles/2005/04/14/kaiser_chiefs_interview.shtml|title=BBC - South Yorkshire - Entertainment - Kaiser Chiefs interview|website=Bbc.co.uk}}</ref>\n\nManager James Sandom was tipped off about the band by [[Drowned in Sound]] founder Sean Adams, who persuaded him to go and see them live. Sandom said: \"I went to see a couple of shows and you were just bombarded by a series of potential hit singles.\" Soon after Sandom became their manager, Kaiser Chiefs signed to [[B-Unique Records]]. [[Atlantic Records]] had also made an offer for the band.<ref name=\"hitquarters.com\"/>\n\n===''Employment'' (2004\u20132005)===\n[[File:Ricky wilson lollapalooza.jpg|thumb|upright|[[Ricky Wilson (singer)|Ricky Wilson]] performing at [[Lollapalooza]] in 2005]]\nIn July 2004, while still relatively unknown inside the UK, Kaiser Chiefs performed their first festival outside the UK at a festival in [[Moscow]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.nme.com/reviews/7580 |title=The Ordinary Boys/Kaiser Chiefs : Liquid Rooms, Edinburgh, Wed, Oct 6 |last1=Sterry |first1=Mike |date=6 December 2004 |publisher=[[NME]] |access-date=9 March 2013}}</ref> headlined by UK band Goldblade. One of the major factors in the band's breakthrough in 2005 was their involvement in the NME Awards Tour at the beginning of the year.<ref name=\"NME\">{{cite web|title=SHOCKWAVES NME AWARDS 2005|url=https://www.nme.com/news/nme-awards/19257|website=Nme.com|access-date=15 June 2011}}</ref> Like [[Coldplay]] and [[Franz Ferdinand (band)|Franz Ferdinand]] before them, their position as opening act proved an influential one, attracting a lot of positive media attention.<ref name=\"hitquarters.com\"/>\n\nThe group's debut album ''[[Employment (album)|Employment]]'' was released in March 2005, being primarily inspired by [[New wave music|new wave]] and [[punk rock]] music of the late 1970s and 1980s.<ref name=\"allmusic\"/> The album was well received by music critics, described as \"thrilling from beginning to end\" and \"quintessentially British, without pretension and most importantly, a whole lot of fun\".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.metacritic.com/music/artists/kaiserchiefs/employment|title=Employment by Kaiser Chiefs|publisher=Metacritic.com|access-date=27 September 2014|archive-date=1 May 2010|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100501121008/http://www.metacritic.com/music/artists/kaiserchiefs/employment|url-status=dead}}</ref> It reached number two on the [[UK albums chart]],<ref>{{cite web|url=http://acharts.us/album/13799|title=Employment Music Charts: Kaiser Chiefs|publisher=Acharts.us|access-date=27 September 2014}}</ref> and was certified five times platinum.<ref>[http://www.bpi.co.uk/platinum/platinumright.asp?rq=search_plat&r_id=32536 British platinum certification for ''Employment''] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20071018124557/http://bpi.co.uk/platinum/platinumright.asp?rq=search_plat&r_id=32536 |date=18 October 2007 }}. [[British Phonographic Industry]]. 17 February 2006. Retrieved 14 September 2007.</ref> In 2005, ''Employment'' was shortlisted for the [[Mercury Prize]], an annual music prize awarded for the best British or Irish album from the previous year. In 2007, the song was [[cover version|covered]] by [[Mark Ronson]] and [[Lily Allen]] for Ronson's album ''[[Version (album)|Version]]''. \"[[I Predict a Riot]]\" soon followed as the album's second release. In 2007, the song was ranked number thirty-six on the ''[[NME]]'' \"Greatest Indie Anthems Ever\" countdown.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nme.com/news/28031 |title=The Greatest Indie Anthems Ever \u2013 countdown continues |website=Nme.com |access-date=27 September 2014 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141115192800/http://www.nme.com/news/28031 |archive-date=15 November 2014 }}</ref> The top twenty singles \"[[Everyday I Love You Less and Less]]\" and \"[[Modern Way]]\" followed in late 2005. They played a hometown concert at the [[First Direct Arena]] in September 2013, being one of the first artists to perform at the new venue.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.itv.com/news/calendar/2012-11-27/leeds-kaiser-chiefs-return-home-to-play-leeds-arena/ |title=Leeds Kaiser Chiefs return home to play Leeds arena &#124; Calendar - ITV News |publisher=Itv.com |date=27 November 2012 |access-date=13 December 2012}}</ref>\n\n===''Education, Education, Education & War'' (2014&ndash;2015)===\nThe band's fifth studio album, titled ''[[Education, Education, Education & War]]'' was released on 31 March 2014.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://music.apple.com/gb/album/education-education-education/780584347 |title=Education, Education, Education & War by Kaiser Chiefs |date=January 2014 |publisher=[[iTunes]] |access-date=6 January 2014}}</ref> ''Education, Education, Education & War'' is the first album featuring the new Kaiser Chiefs drummer, Vijay Mistry, after [[Nick Hodgson]] left the group in December 2012.<ref name=\"Digital Spy\"/> The album was produced by Ben H. Allen III (who has previously worked with [[Gnarls Barkley]], [[Animal Collective]], and [[Deerhunter]]), and recorded at The Maze Studio in [[Atlanta]]. Three songs were co-written with [[Fraser T Smith]] ([[CeeLo Green]], [[Adele]]).<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nme.com/news/kaiser-chiefs/74190 |title=Kaiser Chiefs announce new album \u2013 watch |work=[[NME]] |date=3 December 2013 |access-date=3 January 2014}}</ref><ref name=\"Spin release\">{{cite web|url=https://www.spin.com/2013/12/kaiser-chiefs-education-war-album-trailer-track-list/ |title=Kaiser Chiefs Preview Fifth Album 'Education, Education, Education & War' |work=[[Spin (magazine)|Spin]] |first=Chris |last=Martins |date=3 December 2013 |access-date=3 January 2014}}</ref> It was mixed by [[Michael Brauer]] ([[My Morning Jacket]], [[Coldplay]]) at [[Electric Lady Studios]] in [[New York City]] in November 2013.<ref name=\"ATO Records\">{{cite web|url=http://atorecords.com/featured/kaiser-chiefs-announce-new-album-out-april-1st/ |title=KAISER CHIEFS Announce New Album Out April 1st. Pre-Order Now |publisher=[[ATO Records]] |date=3 December 2013 |access-date=6 January 2014}}</ref> On 5 December 2013, Kaiser Chiefs released a song titled \"Misery Company\" onto their [[SoundCloud]] account. The song had previously been performed live during the band's 2013 summer tour.<ref name=\"Digital Spy\">{{cite web|url=http://www.digitalspy.co.uk/music/news/a537116/kaiser-chiefs-debut-new-song-misery-company-listen.html |title=Kaiser Chiefs debut new song 'Misery Company' - listen |publisher=[[Digital Spy]] |first=Lewis |last=Corner |date=10 December 2013 |access-date=6 January 2013}}</ref><ref name=\"Misery\">{{cite web|url=https://www.nme.com/news/kaiser-chiefs/74333 |title=Kaiser Chiefs unveil new track 'Misery Company' \u2013 listen |work=[[NME]] |date=10 December 2013 |access-date=6 January 2013}}</ref> On 3 January 2014, \"Bows & Arrows\" premiered on ''[[Rolling Stone]]'' magazine's website.\n\n''Education, Education, Education & War'' was toured across Europe and the Americas throughout 2014 and 2015.\n\nIn 2015, they supported the ''[[Foo Fighters]]'' during the South Africa, South America and Ireland legs of their ''[[Sonic Highways World Tour]]''. They also released stand alone single 'Falling Awake' on 27 January 2015 via their YouTube channel.\n\n=== ''Stay Together'' (2016\u20132017) ===\n''[[Stay Together (album)|Stay Together]]'', the band's sixth studio album, was released on 7 October 2016. It was co-written and produced by [[Brian Higgins(producer)|Brian Higgins]], whose production company [[Xenomania]] has worked with the likes of [[Girls Aloud]] and [[Pet Shop Boys]]. The album's name is a reference to the song \"We Stay Together\".<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.radiox.co.uk/artists/kaiser-chiefs/music/listen-kaiser-chiefs-unveil-parachute-single/#hOwt3Dfjk7RI9FQ3.97|title=LISTEN: KAISER CHIEFS UNVEIL PARACHUTE SINGLE {{!}} Radiox.co.uk/|last=Radiox.co.uk|website=Radiox.co.uk|language=en-GB|access-date=13 June 2016|archive-date=9 October 2016|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161009084350/http://www.radiox.co.uk/artists/kaiser-chiefs/music/listen-kaiser-chiefs-unveil-parachute-single/#hOwt3Dfjk7RI9FQ3.97|url-status=dead}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.nme.com/news/kaiser-chiefs/91644|title=Kaiser Chiefs working on new album of love songs |website=Nme.com|language=en-GB|access-date=7 May 2016}}</ref> The first single from the album, \"[[Parachute (Kaiser Chiefs song)|Parachute]]\", was released on 14 June 2016. The second single, \"Hole In My Soul\" was released 18 August 2016 and the third and final single, \"We Stay Together\" was released on 9 December 2016.\n\nThis welcome return to the limelight saw \u2018Stay Together\u2019 push new boundaries to \"take this nondescript sound and stuff it impressively full of sing-a-long hooks, covering topics including monogamy (We Stay Together) and shagging (Good Clean Fun)\" as it marked an entirely new direction for the band, who went for a more dance-oriented sound.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/music/2016/oct/06/kaiser-chiefs-stay-together-review-caroline|title=Kaiser Chiefs: Stay Together review \u2013 nondescript but still full of hooks|website=The Guardian|date=6 October 2016|access-date=17 May 2017|last1=Aroesti|first1=Rachel}}</ref> While ''[[Education, Education, Education and War]]'' was more focused on politics and the perils of war, the Kaiser Chiefs made the album with a focus on love and relationships. Wilson elaborated further on the stark contrast between this last album and 'Stay Together': \"The thing about writing a protest album is it's straightforward. Only an idiot would disagree with you. You're just saying war is bad. But when you start talking about relationships like on this record it's harder because there's a lot more blurred line. There's no right or wrong.\"<ref>{{cite web|last1=Trefor|first1=Cai|title=Kaiser Chiefs' Ricky Wilson Opens Up About New Album Stay Together|url=http://www.gigwise.com/blogs/108272/kaiser-chiefs-stay-together-new-album-parachute-hole-in-my-soul|website=Gigwise|access-date=13 October 2016|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161014061046/http://www.gigwise.com/blogs/108272/kaiser-chiefs-stay-together-new-album-parachute-hole-in-my-soul|archive-date=14 October 2016}}</ref>\n\nThe band performed a string of best of forest shows in the summer of 2016 as part the ''[[Forestry Commission]]'''s ''Forest Live'' series. Stay Together was toured across Europe in early 2017.\n\n===''Duck'' (2018\u20132021)===\nThe band signed with their original label [[Polydor Records]] for their seventh studio album, ''[[Duck (album)|Duck]]'', which released on 26 July 2019. For the album the band reunited with [[Ben H. Allen]], the producer who worked on the band's 2014 album ''[[Education, Education, Education & War]]''. The album was preceded by a UK tour in January and February, including concerts at London's [[O2 Academy Brixton]] and [[O2 Academy Birmingham]], where several new songs from the album were premiered. The band also played a homecoming show at Elland Road in Leeds on 8 June 2019, which was also to celebrate 100 years of [[Leeds United F.C.|Leeds United.]]<ref>{{Cite web |title=Kaiser Chiefs To Celebrate 100 Years Of Leeds United With Elland Road Gig |url=https://www.radiox.co.uk/artists/kaiser-chiefs/celebrate-100-years-leeds-united-elland-road-gig/ |access-date=2024-05-06 |website=Radio X |language=en}}</ref> \n\nThe first single from the album, \"Record Collection\", was released on 23 May 2019, with \"People Know How To Love One Another\" released as a second single on 6 June 2019. \"Golden Oldies\" was released as the third single in October 2019. The album entered at number three in the UK album charts.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.officialcharts.com/artist/15414/kaiser-chiefs/ |title=Kaiser Chiefs |website=Official Charts|access-date=28 March 2020}}</ref> ''Duck'' was later toured around arenas in the UK and Europe in early 2020, with all further touring cancelled due to the [[COVID-19 pandemic]].\n\n===''Kaiser Chiefs' Easy Eighth Album'' (2022\u2013present)===\nIn October 2022, the band announced the single \"How 2 Dance\", the first for [[V2 Records#V2 Benelux|V2 Records]], which was released on 4 November.
