The band consists of twin brothers Rich (bass and backing vocals) and Ryan Meyer (drums and backing vocals), Johnny Stevens (guitar and lead vocals), Matt Kofos (guitar, synthesizer, percussion, and backing vocals) and Mark Schwartz (guitar, synthesizers, keyboards, backing vocals).<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.instagram.com/p/B1McZXYFACk/ |archive-url=https://ghostarchive.org/iarchive/s/instagram/B1McZXYFACk |archive-date=December 24, 2021 |url-access=limited|title=SiriusXM Octane on Instagram: \"I gladly joined the guys in Highly Suspect in their hazing of new band mate Matt. The song \"[[Lydia (Highly Suspect song)|Lydia]]\" was nominated for [[Grammy Award for Best Rock Song|Best Rock Song]].<ref name=\"grammys\">{{cite web |url= http://www.wqlz.com/2015/12/07/grammy-nominations-announced-included-slipknot-sevendust-highly-suspect-and-ghost/ |title=Grammy Nominations Announced; Included Slipknot, Sevendust, Highly Suspect and Ghost|publisher=[[WQLZ]] |date=December 7, 2015|access-date=December 7, 2015 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170719121150/http://www.wqlz.com/2015/12/07/grammy-nominations-announced-included-slipknot-sevendust-highly-suspect-and-ghost/|archive-date=July 19, 2017|url-status=dead}}</ref> Highly Suspect's second studio album,<ref name=\"EW\">{{cite magazine|last1=Vain|first1=Madison|title=Highly Suspect Detail Second Album, 'The Boy Who Died Wolf' \u2013 exclusive |url=http://www.ew.com/article/2016/09/07/highly-suspect-boy-who-died-wolf |magazine=Entertainment Weekly |access-date=November 2, 2016}}</ref> ''[[The Boy Who Died Wolf]]'', was released in 2016. Two singles were released from the album, \"[[My Name Is Human]]\", which topped the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' US [[Mainstream Rock (chart)|Mainstream Rock Songs]] chart, and \"[[Little One (Highly Suspect song)|Little One]]\", which peaked at number 2 on the same chart.<ref>{{cite magazine|url=https://www.billboard.com/artist/6531923/highly-suspect/chart?f=376 |title=Highly Suspect \u2013 Chart history \u2013 Billboard|magazine=Billboard |access-date=January 10, 2019}}</ref> Their third studio album, ''[[MCID]]'', was released in 2019 and their fourth, ''The Midnight Demon Club'', in 2022. Their fifth, ''As Above, So Below'', came out on July 19, 2024.\n\n==History==\n[[File:HighlySuspect October2019.jpg|thumb|Johnny Stevens performs]]\n\n===Formation and ''Mister Asylum'' (2009\u20132015)===\nHighly Suspect began in 2009 as a cover band playing songs by [[Sublime (band)|Sublime]], [[Jimi Hendrix]], and [[Pink Floyd]] in local bars in the Cape Cod area including the British Beer Company, J.R Brody's Roadside Tavern, and Sundancers. They also toured and played a limited number of shows with bands [[10 Years (band)|10 Years]], Monster Truck, and [[Sevendust]].<ref name=\"newyorktimes\">{{cite web|first=Joe|last=Coscarelli|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2016/02/15/arts/music/in-grammy-race-the-band-highly-suspect-marvels-at-its-surprising-double-shot.html?_r=0|title=In Grammy Race, the Band Highly Suspect Marvels at Its Surprising Double Shot |work=[[The New York Times]] |date=February 14, 2016|access-date=February 18, 2016}}</ref> The band all graduated from [[Dennis-Yarmouth Regional High School]] in [[South Yarmouth, Massachusetts]], east of [[Hyannis, Massachusetts|Hyannis]].<ref name=\"enterprisenews\">{{cite web|first=Jay N.|last=Miller |url=http://www.enterprisenews.com/article/20150528/ENTERTAINMENT/150526130|title=MUSIC SCENE: Highly Suspect Revel in the Growing Buzz|work=[[Enterprise News]] |date=March 4, 2015|access-date=June 25, 2015}}</ref>\n\n''First Offense'' was Highly Suspect's first EP, released on July 15, 2009. The EP has \"Life's a Fun Ride\", \"Not Me\", and \"Smile On\", their first written and recorded songs (\"Not Me\" would later appear on the self-titled album ''Highly Suspect''). ''The Gang Lion EP'' was the band's second EP, released on October 3, 2010. It consists of \"Gang Lion\", \"Big Bear\", and \"Then Mickey\", all of which later appeared on their self-titled album, ''Highly Suspect''.\n\n''The Worst Humans'' was the band's third EP, released July 13, 2012. The EP featured the songs \"Bath Salts\", \"Gumshoe\" and \"The Go\". The recordings were available for both physical purchase and digital download on [[Bandcamp]]. The band's fourth EP, ''Black Ocean'', was released in October 2013. It featured two tracks from their debut release as well as three new tracks, \"_ Me Up\", \"Lydia\", and \"Guess What\". The release was jointly produced by Joel Hamilton and [[Joe Duplantier]] of [[Gojira (band)|Gojira]].\n\nTheir self-titled compilation album ''Highly Suspect'' was released in 2011. It contains fourteen tracks, consisting of several new songs as well as previously released but re-recorded songs, like \"Gang Lion\" and \"Then Mickey\". The band toured the [[United States]] in early 2015 with [[Deftones]], [[Chevelle (band)|Chevelle]], [[Halestorm]], [[Catfish and the Bottlemen]] and [[Scott Weiland]] from [[Stone Temple Pilots]].<ref name=\"enterprisenews\" /> The music video for the song \"Lydia\" premiered on [[MTV]].com on June 25, 2015.<ref name=\"MTVbio\">{{cite web|url=http://www.mtv.com/artists/highly-suspect/biography/|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150630043104/http://www.mtv.com/artists/highly-suspect/biography/|url-status=dead|archive-date=June 30, 2015|title=Artist Spotlight: Highly Suspect |work=[[MTV]] |access-date=June 25, 2015}}</ref>\n\nTheir first studio album<ref>{{cite magazine|title=10 New Artists You Need to Know Now|url=https://www.rollingstone.com/music/lists/10-new-artists-you-need-to-know-may-2015-20150526/highly-suspect-20150522|magazine=Rolling Stone|date=May 26, 2015 |access-date=November 2, 2016}}</ref><ref name=\"Loudwire\">{{cite web|last1=Childers|first1=Chad|title=Highly Suspect Reveal 'The Boy Who Died Wolf' Album Details|url=http://loudwire.com/highly-suspect-the-boy-who-died-wolf-album-details-my-name-is-human-single/|website=Loudwire|date=September 7, 2016 |access-date=November 2, 2016}}</ref> ''[[Mister Asylum]]'' was released on July 17, 2015, via [[300 Entertainment]].<ref name=\"billboard\">{{cite magazine |last=Weiner |first=Natalie |date=June 1, 2015 |title=Highly Suspect Debuts Grungy New Track |url=https://www.billboard.com/music/rock/highly-suspect-claudeland-exclusive-mister-asylum-6583101/ |access-date=June 30, 2015 |magazine=[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]}}</ref> The first single from the album, titled \"[[Lydia (Highly Suspect song)|Lydia]]\", peaked at number 4 on the US Mainstream Rock Songs chart and at number 24 on the US [[Alternative Songs]] chart. On July 24, 2015, the album debuted at number 39 on the [[New Zealand Albums Chart]].<ref name=\"nztop40\">{{cite web|url=https://aotearoamusiccharts.co.nz/archive/albums/2015-07-24|title=NZ Top 40 Albums Chart|publisher=[[Recorded Music NZ]]|date=July 27, 2015|access-date=July 24, 2015}}</ref> Highly Suspect was selected for iTunes' 2015 New Artist Spotlight by its editors.<ref name=\"iTunes\">{{cite web|url=https://music.apple.com/us/album/mister-asylum/975018114|title=Highly Suspect on iTunes|work=[[iTunes]]|access-date=June 30, 2015}}</ref> In November, the band released their second single \"Bloodfeather\", which peaked at number 5 on the US Mainstream Rock Songs chart.\n\n===''The Boy Who Died Wolf'' (2016\u20132017)===\n[[File:Highly Suspect 2015.jpg|thumb|Highly Suspect performing at the Village Theatre in [[Davenport, Iowa]] east of [[Iowa City, Iowa|Iowa City]], November 2015]]\nIn June of 2016, Highly Suspect travelled to Bogota Colombia to record their second studio album, again with producer Joel Hamilton.
